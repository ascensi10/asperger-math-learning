# Geometry / Trigonometry — Asperger Tutor Reference

## Teaching Order
1. Shape = spatial object (draw / see first, name later).
2. Length → area → volume (increase in dimension: $1\text{D} \to 2\text{D} \to 3\text{D}$).
3. Angle = amount of rotation (concrete: clock hand).
4. Pythagoras → similarity → trigonometry.
5. Coordinate geometry (bridge to algebra).

## Critical Concretization Points

**Area vs. Perimeter (frequently confused):** perimeter = sum of side lengths (the outside); area = surface covered (the inside). For a $3 \times 4$ rectangle:
$$\text{Perimeter} = 2(3 + 4) = 14, \qquad \text{Area} = 3 \cdot 4 = 12$$
Unit clue: area units are squared ($\text{cm}^2$) because two dimensions are multiplied.

**Pythagoras (why $a^2 + b^2$):** In a right triangle, the squares of the sides are actual areas; explain with a visual:
$$a^2 + b^2 = c^2 \qquad (c = \text{hypotenuse — longest side})$$
Example: $3^2 + 4^2 = 9 + 16 = 25 = 5^2 \Rightarrow c = 5$.

**Trigonometry (as a ratio, not memorization):** In a right triangle:
$$\sin\theta = \frac{\text{opposite}}{\text{hypotenuse}}, \qquad \cos\theta = \frac{\text{adjacent}}{\text{hypotenuse}}, \qquad \tan\theta = \frac{\text{opposite}}{\text{adjacent}}$$
Even if the triangle scales up, these ratios remain constant (similarity). Each is just a single ratio — not a formula to blindly memorize.

**Angle = rotation:** $90^\circ$ = quarter turn, $180^\circ$ = half turn, $360^\circ$ = full turn.

## Common Working-Memory Errors

| Error | Why it happens | Checkpoint |
|---|---|---|
| Area / perimeter confusion | Two concepts at once | Ask for the unit: squared ($\text{cm}^2$) or flat ($\text{cm}$)? |
| Misidentifying the hypotenuse | Visual shift | "Longest side, opposite to the right angle" |
| Mixing up ratios | Remains too abstract | Draw the triangle every single time, label opposite / adjacent |
| Unit conversion ($\text{cm} \to \text{m}$) | Too many steps | Do the conversion on a separate line |
